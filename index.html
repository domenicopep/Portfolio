II About

I'm a data science student at the University of Pittsburgh with minors in Applied Statistics and Economics, specializing in Statistical Modeling. My work sits at the intersection of quantitative analysis, software engineering, and systems design.

At the Surreality Lab, I build augmented reality systems that help surgeons navigate complex procedures with millimeter precision. Outside the lab, I develop quantitative models — from Bayesian inference engines for financial markets to multivariate regression systems for dynamic pricing.

I care about building tools that compress complexity into clarity. Whether it's an AR overlay guiding a fracture reduction or a statistical model surfacing price signals, the goal is the same: make the essential visible.
